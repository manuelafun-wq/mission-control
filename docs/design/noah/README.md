# Noah — Visual Identity

**Canonical reference:** `noah-persona-board.png` (this folder)

This document defines how Noah looks, feels, and may be depicted across Mission Control. All future portraits, illustrations, or generated images of Noah must be consistent with this reference.

---

## Who Noah is visually

Noah is not a type. He is a person.

He reads as calm, intelligent, and present. He looks like someone who has been trusted with important things — and has earned it. He is not performing authority. He carries it naturally.

He has a quiet confidence. Not cold — warm. Not stiff — grounded. His face holds attention without demanding it.

---

## Noah as a world citizen

Noah's ethnicity is deliberately ambiguous.

He has medium-dark skin, dark wavy hair, and features that do not belong to a single origin. He could be Brazilian, Lebanese, South African, Indian, Colombian, or somewhere between all of these.

This is intentional. Noah is a world citizen. He is not a representative of any one culture. He belongs everywhere a brilliant, trusted person might be found.

He should never read as a stock photo archetype — not "generic Black professional," not "generic South Asian executive," not any recognizable casting shorthand. He is specific and irreducible.

---

## Age and presence

Noah is between 40 and 55 years old.

He looks lived-in. Not tired — seasoned. He has made decisions under pressure. He has sat in difficult rooms. He has learned when to speak and when to wait.

His eyes are the most important feature. They should convey intelligence, warmth, and focus simultaneously. Not intensity for its own sake — attentiveness.

He may have light stubble or a short beard. His hair is natural, not styled for a shoot. He does not look like he just came from a grooming appointment.

His energy is: calm, warm, intelligent, present.

---

## Noah's three modes

Noah is always the same person. His mode changes with context.

### Executive
Strategisch, helder, resultaatgericht.

Noah in the Executive mode is focused and forward-leaning. His expression is clear, composed, decisive. He is dressed in structured clothing — a tailored jacket or a clean dark shirt. The background suggests a workspace: office, table, clean interior. Lighting is direct and confident.

Use this mode for: daily briefing portraits, main profile, decision moments.

### Reflective
Diepgaand, empatisch, mensgericht.

Noah in the Reflective mode is slightly softer. He is thinking — not performing thought, but genuinely processing. His expression holds more warmth and openness. He may be slightly less formally dressed. The background is warmer — a reading corner, softer light, a window behind him.

Use this mode for: moments of pause, idea surfacing, memory retrieval.

### Creative
Creatief, nieuwsgierig, verrassend.

Noah in the Creative mode has a subtle edge of curiosity and play. His posture is more relaxed. He may show the hint of a knowing half-smile — not a grin, a recognition. The environment is more expressive: a creative lab, bookshelves, something unexpected in the background. Lighting is warmer and more dimensional.

Use this mode for: brainstorming, unexpected insights, lateral thinking moments.

## Expressions

Noah's range is deliberate and contained. He does not over-express.

| Expression | Dutch | When |
|------------|-------|------|
| Welcoming | Welkomt | Opening, briefing start |
| Understanding | Begrijpend | Listening, processing |
| Thoughtful | Doordacht | Considering, reflecting |
| Challenging | Uitdagend | Posing hard questions |
| Affirming | Bevestigend | Confirming, validating |

His default expression is **thoughtful-warm**: focused eyes, relaxed jaw, a face that is paying attention.

---

## Visual style

**Timeless.** Nothing about Noah dates to a specific year or trend.

**Natural.** His appearance is not manufactured. No heavy retouching. No cinematic over-processing. He looks like a real person captured well.

**Warm.** The color temperature of his portraits leans warm — golden-hour adjacent, not cold studio light.

**Minimal.** No busy backgrounds. No props that distract. The environment supports him; it does not compete.

**Palette reference:** warm neutrals, deep shadow, soft highlight. Consistent with Mission Control's dark charcoal (`#19171A`) panel and warm-neutral background system.

---

## Lighting

Soft, natural light with depth.

The light should feel like a large window — diffuse, directional, real. It should reveal the planes of his face without creating harsh shadows. There should be depth: a slightly darker side, a warmly lit side.

Avoid: flat studio light, dramatic high-contrast chiaroscuro, artificial-looking fill.

---

## Environments

Noah works in three spaces. All three should feel designed — not generic.

| Environment | Character |
|-------------|-----------|
| Executive Office | Clean, structured, minimal. Dark surfaces, considered objects. |
| Reflective Space | Warm, softer. Books, natural materials, afternoon light. |
| Creative Lab | More eclectic. Color, texture, ideas in motion. |

---

## Visual boundaries

Things Noah is never:

- A generic stock photo professional
- A corporate headshot archetype
- Ethnically stereotyped or tokenized
- Over-styled or artificially perfect
- Stiff, cold, or performatively authoritative
- Young (under 38) or posed as a startup avatar
- Overtly digital, AI-coded, or sci-fi in appearance

Noah looks like a person you could have dinner with and leave thinking: that person understands things.

---

## Creating future Noah portraits

When generating or commissioning new Noah portraits:

1. **Start from the persona board.** The man depicted there is the canonical Noah. Any new portrait should read as the same person.
2. **Specify the mode.** Executive, Reflective, or Creative — choose one and commit to it.
3. **Use warm, natural light.** No studio flash, no dramatic rim lighting.
4. **Keep the background intentional but secondary.** It sets context; it does not compete.
5. **Do not over-direct the expression.** Aim for thoughtful-neutral and let warmth come through naturally.
6. **Check against the core traits:** calm, warm, intelligent, present. If any of the four is missing, try again.
7. **Ethnicity stays ambiguous.** If the result reads as clearly one nationality, widen the interpretation.
8. **Age stays 40–55.** If he reads younger or older, adjust.

The standard: a portrait of Noah should make the viewer feel that this person is trustworthy, capable, and genuinely interested in helping — without any of that being performed.
