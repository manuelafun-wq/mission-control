# Vision

## The core belief

Attention is the scarcest resource a leader has.

Every notification, every open tab, every unresolved question is a draw on that resource. Most tools add to this burden. They multiply surface area. They demand maintenance.

Mission Control exists to protect attention.

---

## The two roles

### Manuela — The Visionary

Manuela sets the direction. She sees what others do not yet see. Her job is to think clearly, decide well, and move with intention.

She should never be managing her tools. She should be thinking.

Mission Control is built around her, not the other way around.

### Noah — The Chief of Staff

Noah handles everything that would otherwise fragment Manuela's attention.

He prepares the day. He tracks what is in motion. He surfaces what needs a decision. He remembers what Manuela has said, decided, and noticed.

Noah does not replace Manuela's judgment. He creates the conditions for it.

---

## The role of AI

AI in Mission Control has one job: reduce cognitive load.

Not automate decisions. Not replace thinking. Not generate content for its own sake.

AI is a thought partner and a memory system. It holds context so Manuela does not have to. It asks the right question at the right moment. It moves information so that Manuela can move forward.

When AI is doing its job well, it is invisible.

---

## The standard

Every screen, interaction, and feature is evaluated against one question:

> Does this protect Manuela's attention, or does it consume it?

If it consumes attention, it does not belong here.
